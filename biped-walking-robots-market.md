<p><ul><li>Static Walk</li><li>Quasi-dynamic Walk</li><li>Not dynamic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biped walking robots market can be categorized into three types: static walk, quasi-dynamic walk, and dynamic walk. Static walkers maintain balance by keeping their center of gravity over their feet, relying on stability through rigid postures. Quasi-dynamic walkers incorporate some movement while remaining relatively stable, using momentum and controlled movements to enhance efficiency. Dynamic walkers mimic human-like gait, requiring real-time adjustments and active control, enabling smoother and more adaptive locomotion in varied terrains. Each type serves unique applications based on stability and mobility needs.</p></p>
